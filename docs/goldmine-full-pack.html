AI workflows

Three repeatable production recipes.

1

Faceless short workflow

Trend question -> 5 hooks -> 80-word script -> voiceover -> 4-6 visual scenes -> captions -> title variants -> post.

Tools: ChatGPT/Claude/Gemini, CapCut, ElevenLabs or native TTS, stock footage, Canva, a simple spreadsheet tracker.

2

AI influencer affiliate workflow

Character brief -> product angle -> image batch -> reel script -> caption -> link routing -> email capture -> weekly product roundup.

Tools: image model, Canva, scheduled posting, affiliate network, simple landing page, newsletter tool.

3

UGC ad concept workflow

Brand page -> product pain points -> 10 hooks -> 3 scripts -> avatar or rough visual -> landing-page angle -> sample pitch.

Tools: LLM, screenshot capture, CapCut, avatar tool if needed, a one-page portfolio, and a direct outreach list.

Keep the workflow boring. The content can be creative, but the production system should be repetitive enough that you can run it when your motivation is low.

Channel starter pack

Turn the idea into a public surface.

Naming formulas

Use names that reveal the promise.

  • The Daily [Outcome]: The Daily Automator, The Daily AI Ad.
  • [Niche] Lab: Creator Tools Lab, AI Career Lab.
  • [Audience] Shortcuts: Freelancer Shortcuts, Founder Shortcuts.
  • [Problem] Fix: Content Fix, Outreach Fix, Resume Fix.
Bio formula

Simple is better.

"I test [tools/workflows/products] that help [audience] get [outcome] without [pain]. Follow for [format] every [frequency]."

Example: "I test AI workflows that help solo creators publish faster without hiring a team. Follow for one useful workflow every day."

First five posts
  1. The problem: why your niche is painful right now.
  2. The shortcut: one small workflow or tool demo.
  3. The comparison: manual way vs assisted way.
  4. The teardown: show one account, brand, or workflow.
  5. The invite: ask people what they want you to test next.

The first five posts are not meant to go viral. They are meant to establish a repeatable format, so your next fifty have somewhere to go.

Hook bank

A good hook creates a tiny open loop. It should make the reader think: "Wait, what is the thing?" Use these as starting points, then make them specific to your niche.

Monetization map

Views are not the business. The route is.

Fastest to test

Affiliate or lead magnet

Best when your content naturally recommends tools, products, platforms, or templates. Add a simple link destination and track clicks before optimizing anything else.

Metric to watch: saves, profile clicks, link clicks, email signups.

Best for builders

Service or workflow template

Best when your content shows you can solve an operational problem. Turn one demo into a paid setup, audit, or downloadable workflow.

Metric to watch: replies, DMs, booked calls, template purchases.

Best long-term

Newsletter or digital product

Best when your audience wants repeated insight. Use content to gather emails, then package guides, prompts, dashboards, or deeper research.

Metric to watch: opt-in rate, reply rate, paid conversion.

Best after traction

Sponsorships

Best once you have consistent reach in a clear category. Do not wait for huge follower counts if your audience is specific and buyers care about it.

Metric to watch: niche clarity, average reach, audience proof.

Pick one primary route now. You can add more later. A scattered monetization plan creates scattered content.

Final notes

Build curiosity. Keep your claims clean.

Do this
  • Show your process, not just the result.
  • Use real examples and honest limitations.
  • Keep one content promise for at least 7 days.
  • Track clicks, replies, saves, and DMs.
  • Turn repeated questions into products or services.
Avoid this
  • Do not promise guaranteed income.
  • Do not fake results or screenshots.
  • Do not copy creators without adding your own angle.
  • Do not start five pages at once.
  • Do not confuse views with a business model.

The Goldmine rule

A good idea should make you curious enough to test it today and disciplined enough to measure it tomorrow. If it only feels exciting in your head, it is not a play yet. Turn it into one post, one link, one offer, or one workflow.

This guide is educational and does not guarantee income. Use it as a practical starting map, then let real audience behavior decide what deserves your next week.
