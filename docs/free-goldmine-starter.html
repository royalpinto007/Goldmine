Your quick idea map.

Choose by strength

  1. Pick analysis if you enjoy explaining.
  2. Pick trends if you move fast.
  3. Pick product roundups if you like useful lists.

Choose by proof

  1. Can you post it three times this week?
  2. Can people save or share it?
  3. Can it lead to a checklist, link page, or template?
Choose the one you could post for 7 days without getting bored. The best idea is usually the one you can repeat calmly, not the one that sounds the fanciest.

One simple AI workflow

Turn one topic into four assets.

Use this before buying tools or building automation. The goal is one useful post, then one useful remix, then one small next step.

Input

  • Topic: a player debate, match question, or fan problem.
  • Audience: casual fans, fantasy players, training fans, or creators.
  • Format: reel, carousel, poll, or newsletter.

Prompt

Give me 5 football content angles for [topic]. For each angle, include a hook, a 30-second script, one visual idea, one caption, and one safe monetization path. Avoid copyrighted clips and fake income claims.

Example output

  1. Hook: This goal started 18 seconds before the shot.
  2. Short script: explain the build-up in plain English.
  3. Carousel: show claim, example, takeaway, counterpoint, question.
  4. CTA: Want the checklist I used for this breakdown?

Publishing checklist

  1. Use your own wording.
  2. Fact-check the claim.
  3. Avoid copyrighted footage.
  4. Track saves, replies, shares, and profile clicks.

One topic becomes four assets

  1. Reel: one 35-second explanation.
  2. Carousel: one 5-slide breakdown.
  3. Poll: one question to collect comments.
  4. Newsletter note: one paragraph with the useful takeaway.

Copy prompt to start

Create a football content pack about [topic]. Give me a hook, a short video script, a carousel outline, a poll question, a newsletter paragraph, and one soft CTA. Keep it factual and avoid income promises.

CTA examples

  1. Want the checklist I used?
  2. Should I make a template for this?
  3. Comment a player or match and I will make a version.
If the hook feels generic, the post will feel generic. Make the angle specific before making it pretty.

7-day starter path

Your first test week.

Days 1 to 3

  • Pick one idea lane.
  • Write 10 hooks.
  • Publish 3 simple posts in the same format.
  • Save comments and questions.

Days 4 to 7

  • Turn the best post into a second format.
  • Create one useful link destination.
  • Add a small CTA.
  • Decide whether to repeat, refine, or stop.
